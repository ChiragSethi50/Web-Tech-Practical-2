4 Most Successful Movie franchises



Chirag Sethi

Last Updated Mar 01, 2022

Marvel Cinematic Universe — $25.56B

A franchise that is so far ahead with the box office numbers, that it's
nearly impossible to take its place. It certainly helps there are 27
movies in the franchise, but it doesn't mean each of those movies aren't
indeed commercial successes. From Iron Man in 2008 to Spider-man: No Way
Home in 2021, the Marvel Cinematic Universe keeps proving there are more
stories to tell. Even though some are afraid the universe is getting too
complicated and nonsensical, the $25.56B mark is a sign they won't stop
anytime soon.

Marvel Cinematic Universe

Star Wars — $10.31B

Nine movies grossing over $10.31B and all nominated for the Academy
Awards at some point. This is as clear an indication of both critical
and commercial success as can be. We wonder if it was George Lucas'
intention to make a pop-cultural phenomenon out of a sci-fi movie,
something never seen before the 70s. With 3 separate film trilogies and
another planned trilogy on the way.

Star Wars

Spider-man — $9.25B

It's only recently that the Spider-Man franchise took Harry Potter's
third place as the highest grossing franchise. It's all thanks to the
release of the latest Spider-Man: No Way Home movie, which has grossed
over $1.53 billion worldwide. Although Kevin Feige confirmed that they
are developing a fourth MCU Spider-Man movie, it's hard to say how it's
going to work out given the ongoing conflict between Disney and Sony. No
matter the drama and disputes, Spider-Man still ranks third with over
$9.25B grossed.

Spider-man

Harry Potter — $9.20B

The success of the Harry Potter book series was a big indicator of how
the films would do when Warner Bros. decided to develop them. Ten movies
have managed to gross over $9.20B in the worldwide box office. All four
directors that worked on the movies (Chris Columbus, Alfonso Cuarón,
Mike Newell, and David Yates) put their unique spin on the style and the
storytelling. The Fantastic Beasts is a great spin-off prequel, but we
would also definitely love to see a series dedicated to the Marauders'
era at Hogwarts.
